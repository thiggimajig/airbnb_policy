Welcome to the Florence Airbnb Policy Tool

This web app tool was built as part of a masters thesis project with the Economics and Statistics Department at the University of Florence (Universit√† degli studi di Firenze). The primary use case is for policy makers researching their options when it comes to regulating short term rentals. It however can be very useful as an educational and advocacy tool for concerned residents as well. The future hopes for this project is to build out a policy tool for cities and small towns all over the world especially as a preventative measure against overtourism. The tool is organized in three main parts based off of three guiding verbs: Assess, Analyse, Act.

First, on the Assess the Housing Situation Tab:
You can learn about the current housing situation in Florence which includes census data on population and renters.
Then, navigate to the Policy Analyser Tab:
Here you can analyse the current airbnb listings in the city of Florence, with no "proposed" policies applied.
Then you can click into each of the three proposed policies to see how they would impact the city and its airbnb listings
Finally, on the Take Action Tab:
You can get involved, advocate for policies, and connect with a global community of activists.
Keep in mind that the maps are interactive and clickable!
